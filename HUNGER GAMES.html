About Event

“Let your plans be dark and impenetrable as night, and when you move, fall like a thunderbolt.” The event, Quarter Quell intends to bring together astute and ardent students from various colleges for a phenomenal technical milestone. The event, based on the themes of Hunger Games, the sci-fi dystopian class, and Clash of Clans, the freemium mobile strategy video game aims to make coding along with logical reasoning accessible and fun by breaking down complex concepts into engaging challenges that simulate real world scenarios. Our purpose is to help revolutionize today's perception of technology and to fuel the advancements in various other sectors by providing the future pillars of our society-the youth, a platform to set rational solutions to the abundant world problems. Happy Hunger Games! And may the codes be ever in your favor.

About MLSC

Microsoft Learn Student Chapter is a technical society which is dedicated to elevate the coding culture of Thapar Institute of Engineering and Technology by helping students bridge the gap between theory and practice, mentoring and motivating them to refine their critical thinking, logical reasoning and coding skills. MLSC was formed with the objective of providing students a dynamic platform which feeds their curiosity and develops their technical skills. It aims to create a community of versatile enthusiasts with progressive mindset, promoting holistic development which includes injecting soft skills as well. We provide guidance, technical trainings, project guidance that help students here to grow their knowledge in a peer-to-peer learning environment and build solutions for local businesses and their community. MLSC effectuates it by organizing numerous technology driven events including tech meetups and Hackathons and promoting team building. In the long run, it plans to cultivate and nurture young minds of TIET to a magnitude where they are proficient enough to compete amongst the nonpareil, thus upholding the name of TIET. Also, we are now MICROSOFT LEARN STUDENT AMBASSADORS.
